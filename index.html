About Me

I am currently a fourth-year senior at the New Brunswick Campus of Rutgers University. In May, I will be graduating with degrees in Biochemistry, Chemistry, and Neuroscience. In July, I will be graduating from the Rutgers Coding Bootcamp that is also hosted at the New Brunswick Campus. At Rutgers, I work as a Research Assistant for the Center of Cognitive Science and am doing an independent thesis on the effects of sleep deprivation. I am hoping to, one day, collaborate my knowledge of the brain as well as my coding skillset to work in the field of artifical intelligence.

Lightweight Coding

Throughout my time with coding, I have found that simplicity is best. Something I am particularly interested in, and is showcased in my code, is to create complex effects with simple, easy-to-read, and easy-to-manipulate code.

Science and Research

Through my experiences as a neuroscience major and a Research Assistant at the Rutgers Center for Cognitive Science, I have gotten a thorough understanding of how the brain, and more importantly, how people work and operate. I put this into consideration for every piece of application that I code. In order to create a successful application, I feel that you must understand for whom you are creating the application.

Diversified Coding

In college, I took a course in Matlab, which is a multi-paradigm numerical computing environment. This was my introduction into coding in general and I believe that it has been extremely beneficial in teaching me logic that is required for coding in any language. I am currently well-versed in HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap frameworks, Javascript, jQuery, and am in the process of teaching myself: C, Objective-C, and Python.

Target Audience

My mindset before creating any app is to know and understand the population of people that will be utilizing my software. I feel that successful applications are those that cater to the user and respond productively to feedback.

My Portfolio


Above is my most recent project, an app geared towards Rutgers students that allows them to create hangouts with each other via a computer or on a mobile device. I was responsible for creating the app's user interface and was inspired by other applications that were created by Apple for their OSX and iOS devices. I loved how their apps felt minimalistic and unobtrusive yet still maintained a high sense of responsiveness and fluidity for the user. I tried my best at emulating this type of coding into my project. I felt as though the greatest way that I could approach the creation of an app would be to start simple and to execute it extremely well.
